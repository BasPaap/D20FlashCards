<p>You can keep your balance while traversing narrow or treacherous surfaces. You can also dive, flip, jump, and roll, avoiding attacks and confusing your opponents.</p> 
<p><a name="acrobatics-modifiers"></a></p>

<h3><a name="TOC-Common-Uses"></a>Common Uses</h3> 
<p>The Acrobatics skill has three distinct uses:</p> 
<ol> 
 <li><a href="#cross-narrow-surface">Cross Narrow Surfaces/Uneven Ground</a></li> 
 <li><a href="#move-through-threatened-area">Move Through Threatened Squares</a></li> 
 <li><a href="#jumping-and-falling">Jumping and Falling</a></li> 
</ol> 
<p><a name="cross-narrow-surface"></a></p> 
<h4><a name="TOC-Cross-Narrow-Surfaces-Uneven-Ground"></a>Cross Narrow Surfaces/Uneven Ground</h4> 
<p>First, you can use Acrobatics to move on narrow surfaces and uneven ground without falling. A successful check allows you to move at half speed across such surfaces—only one check is needed per round. Use the following table to determine the base DC, which is then modified by the Acrobatics skill modifiers noted below. While you are using Acrobatics in this way, you are considered <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Flat-Footed"> flat-footed</a> and lose your <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/basics-ability-scores/ability-scores#TOC-Dexterity-Dex-" rel="nofollow">Dexterity</a> bonus to your AC (if any). If you take damage while using Acrobatics, you must immediately make another Acrobatics check at the same DC to avoid falling or being knocked prone.</p>

<p style="border-bottom: solid thin;font-weight: bold">Cross Narrow Surface DC Modifiers</p> 
<ul> 
 <li><b>Balancing Pole</b>: Using a balancing pole while traversing a narrow surface grants a +1 <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/basics-ability-scores/glossary#TOC-Circumstance-Bonus">circumstance bonus</a> to the Acrobatics check.</li> 
</ul> 
<p><a name="move-through-threatened-area"></a></p> 
<h4><a name="TOC-Move-Through-Threatened-Squares"></a>Move Through Threatened Squares</h4> 
<p>In addition, you can move through a threatened square without provoking an <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Attacks-of-Opportunity" rel="nofollow">attack of opportunity</a> from an enemy by using Acrobatics. When moving in this way, you move at half speed. You can move at full speed by increasing the DC of the check by 10. You cannot use Acrobatics to move past foes if your speed is reduced due to carrying a medium or heavy load or wearing medium or heavy armor. If an ability allows you to move at full speed under such conditions, you can use Acrobatics to move past foes. You can use Acrobatics in this way while prone, but doing so requires a full-round action to move 5 feet, and the DC is increased by 5. If you attempt to move through an enemy’s space and fail the check, you lose the move action and provoke an attack of opportunity.</p>

<p><a name="jumping-and-falling"></a></p> 
<h4><a name="TOC-Jumping-and-Falling"></a>Jumping and Falling</h4> 
<p>Finally, you can use the Acrobatics skill to make jumps or to soften a fall. The base DC to make a jump is equal to the distance to be crossed (if horizontal) or four times the height to be reached (if vertical). These DCs double if you do not have at least 10 feet of space to get a running start. The only Acrobatics modifiers that apply are those concerning the surface you are jumping from. If you fail this check by 4 or less, you can attempt a DC 20 <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Reflex" rel="nofollow">Reflex</a> save to grab hold of the other side after having missed the jump. If you fail by 5 or more, you fail to make the jump and fall (or land prone, in the case of a vertical jump).</p>

<p style="border-bottom: solid thin;font-weight: bold">Jump DC Modifiers</p> 
<p><a name="modifiers-faster-movement"></a></p> 
<ul> 
 <li><b>Faster Base Movement</b>: Creatures with a base land speed above 30 feet receive a +4 racial bonus on Acrobatics checks made to jump for every 10 feet of their speed above 30 feet. Creatures with a base land speed below 30 feet receive a –4 racial bonus on Acrobatics checks made to jump for every 10 feet of their speed below 30 feet. No jump can allow you to exceed your maximum movement for the round.</li> 
 <li><b>Running Jump</b>: For a running jump, the result of your Acrobatics check indicates the distance traveled in the jump (and if the check fails, the distance at which you actually land and fall prone). Halve this result for a standing long jump to determine where you land. Also, see Pole.</li> 
 <li><b>Pole</b>: If you use a pole as part of a running jump, you gain a +2 <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/basics-ability-scores/glossary#TOC-Circumstance-Bonus">circumstance bonus</a> on your Acrobatics check (but must let go of the pole in the process).</li> 
 <li><b>Falling</b>: When you deliberately fall any distance, even as a result of a missed jump, a DC 15 Acrobatics skill check allows you to ignore the first 10 feet fallen, although you still end up prone if you take damage from a fall. See <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/environment/environmental-rules#TOC-Falling">Falling Damage</a> for more details.)</li> 
</ul> 
<p style="font-weight: bold;border-bottom: solid thin">Special Situation: Diving or Jumping into Water</p> 
<p class="source"><b>Source</b> <a href="http://www.opengamingstore.com/products/cerulean-seas-campaign-setting-and-undersea-sourcebook" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cerulean Seas © 2010 Alluria Publishing. All rights reserved</a>.</p> 
<p>The rules presented here for diving or jumping into water are from a Pathfinder 3rd Party Publisher and are not part of this game rules created by Paizo. Consult your GM to see if these rules are allowed or in use in his or her campaign.</p>

Water 30 feet deep is sufficient for a dive from any height. However, the DC of the check increases by 5 for every 50 feet of the dive. <b>Table: Diving</b> summarizes these rules.</p>

    <td class="text">2d3 <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Nonlethal-Damage">nonlethal</a> + 17d6</td> 
   </tr> 
   <tr> 
    <td class="distance text">240 ft.</td> 
    <td class="distance text">30 ft.</td> 
    <td class="number">35</td> 
    <td class="text">2d3 <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Nonlethal-Damage">nonlethal</a> + 20d6*</td> 
   </tr> 
  </tbody> 
 </table> 
</div> 
<p class="source">*Maximum falling damage.</p> 
<p><b>Action</b>: None. An Acrobatics check is made as part of another action or as a reaction to a situation.</p> 
<h3><a name="TOC-Modifiers"></a>Modifiers</h3> 
<ul> 
 <li><b>Skills</b> If you have 3 or more ranks in Acrobatics, you gain a +3 dodge bonus to AC when fighting defensively instead of the usual +2, and a +6 dodge bonus to AC when taking the total defense action instead of the usual +4.</li> 
 <li><b>Feats</b> If you have the Acrobatic feat, you get a +2 bonus on all Acrobatics checks. If you have 10 or more ranks in Acrobatics, the bonus increases to +4.</li> 
</ul> 
<h4><a name="TOC-Acrobatics-Unchained"></a>Acrobatics Unchained</h4> 
<p class="source"><b>Source</b> <a title="Pathfinder Unchained" href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/1601257155/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;creativeASIN=1601257155&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=httpwwwd20pfs-20&amp;linkId=SUELYCMD4O5LZ3U6" target="_blank" rel="noopener">PFU</a></p> 
<p><b>About This Section</b> Optionally, a character who reaches 5, 10, 15, or 20 ranks in a skill unlocks various bonuses and abilities unique to that skill. The unchained rogue uses these rules extensively, but others can gain access to them with a new feat.</p> 
<p>In this system, characters unlock additional abilities when they attain 5, 10, 15, and 20 ranks in a skill. The skill unlocks system interfaces with the <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/classes/unchained-classes/rogue-unchained">unchained rogue</a> to make the <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/classes/core-classes/rogue">rogue</a> the true master of skills.</p> 
<p>Skill unlocks give characters new abilities and ways to use their skills upon reaching 5, 10, 15, and 20 ranks in a skill. Any character with the <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/feats/general-feats/signature-skill-general">Signature Skill</a> feat can earn skill unlocks for a single skill, and they are a prime feature of the revised version of the <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/classes/core-classes/rogue">rogue</a> who uses her rogue&#8217;s edge ability to gain skill unlocks for several of her most iconic skills. Alternatively, you might make skill unlocks a universal part of the game, but you should be aware they add significant power and flexibility to skills, so giving them for free to all classes would grant power boosts to other highly skilled classes such as the investigator and <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/classes/core-classes/bard">bard</a>, particularly in comparison to the <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/classes/core-classes/rogue">rogue</a>. Another alternative is to eliminate access to the <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/feats/general-feats/signature-skill-general">Signature Skill</a> feat, limiting skill unlocks to <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/classes/core-classes/rogue">rogues</a> and <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/classes/core-classes/rogue">rogues</a> alone.</p> 
<p>With sufficient ranks in Acrobatics, you earn the following.<a name="Unchained-Skill-Unlock"></a></p> 
<div style="margin-left: 20px"> 
 <p><b>5 Ranks</b>: You can move at normal speed through a <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Threatened-Squares">threatened</a> square without provoking an <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Attacks-of-Opportunity">attack of opportunity</a> by increasing the DC of the check by 5 (instead of by 10). You aren&#8217;t denied your <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/basics-ability-scores/ability-scores#TOC-Dexterity-Dex-">Dexterity</a> bonus when attempting Acrobatics checks with DCs of 20 or lower.</p> 
 <p><b>10 Ranks</b>: You can attempt an Acrobatics check at a –10 penalty and use the result as your <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Combat-Maneuver-Defense">CMD</a> against <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Trip">trip</a> maneuvers. You can also attempt an Acrobatics check at a –10 penalty in place of a <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Reflex">Reflex</a> save to avoid falling. You must choose to use this ability before the <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Trip">trip</a> attempt or <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Reflex">Reflex</a> save is rolled. With a successful DC 20 Acrobatics check, you treat an unintentional fall as 10 feet shorter plus 10 feet for every 10 by which you exceed the DC, and treat an intentional fall as 10 feet shorter for every 10 by which you exceed the DC.</p> 
 <p><b>15 Ranks</b>: You do not provoke <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/combat#TOC-Attacks-of-Opportunity">attacks of opportunity</a> when standing up from <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/conditions#TOC-Prone">prone</a>.</p> 
 <p><b>20 Ranks</b>: You double the result of any Acrobatics check when jumping and never fall <a href="https://www.d20pfsrd.com/gamemastering/conditions#TOC-Prone">prone</a> at the end of a fall as long as you remain conscious.</p> 
</div> 
<div> 
 <p> Time Machine! 
 </div> 
 <div style="padding: 10px"> 
  <p>The Pathfinder Roleplaying Game merged the Balance and Jump skills from its predecessor into the Acrobatics skill. While this has been mostly good, in the transition some information seems to have been left behind.</p> 
  <p><b>Note</b>: The information below is <b>NOT</b> official. However, you may find it helpful in some situations to consider how the rules were previously written. Use at your GMs discretion.</p> 
  <h4><a name="TOC-Long-Jump"></a>Long Jump</h4> 
  <p>A long jump is a horizontal jump, made across a gap like a chasm or stream. At the midpoint of the jump, you attain a vertical height equal to one-quarter of the horizontal distance.</p> 
  <p>If your check succeeds, you land on your feet at the far end. If you fail the check by less than 5, you don’t clear the distance, but you can make a DC 15 Reflex save to grab the far edge of the gap. You end your movement grasping the far edge. If that leaves you dangling over a chasm or gap, getting up requires a move action and a DC 15 Climb check.</p> 
  <h4><a name="TOC-High-Jump"></a>High Jump</h4> 
  <div style="font-size: 14px"> 
   <table border="1" cellpadding="5"> 
    <tbody> 
     <tr> 
      <th class="text">Creature Size</th> 
      <th class="text">Vertical Reach</th> 
     </tr> 
     <tr> 
      <td class="text">Colossal</td> 
      <td class="distance text">128 ft.</td> 
     </tr> 
     <tr> 
      <td class="text">Gargantuan</td> 
      <td class="distance text">64 ft.</td> 
     </tr> 
     <tr> 
      <td class="text">Huge</td> 
      <td class="distance text">32 ft.</td> 
     </tr> 
     <tr> 
      <td class="text">Large</td> 
      <td class="distance text">16 ft.</td> 
     </tr> 
     <tr> 
      <td class="text">Medium</td> 
      <td class="distance text">8 ft.</td> 
     </tr> 
     <tr> 
      <td class="text">Small</td> 
      <td class="distance text">4 ft.</td> 
     </tr> 
     <tr> 
      <td class="text">Tiny</td> 
      <td class="distance text">2 ft.</td> 
     </tr> 
     <tr> 
      <td class="text">Diminutive</td> 
      <td class="distance text">1 ft.</td> 
     </tr> 
     <tr> 
      <td class="text">Fine</td> 
      <td class="text">½ ft.</td> 
     </tr> 
    </tbody> 
   </table> 
  </div> 
  <p>A high jump is a vertical leap made to reach a ledge high above or to grasp something overhead.</p> 
  <p>If you jumped up to grab something, a successful check indicates that you reached the desired height. If you wish to pull yourself up, you can do so with a move action and a DC 15 Climb check. If you fail the Acrobatics check to jump, you do not reach the height, and you land on your feet in the same spot from which you jumped. As with a long jump, the DC is doubled if you do not get a running start of at least 20 feet.</p> 
  <p>Obviously, the difficulty of reaching a given height varies according to the size of the character or creature. The maximum vertical reach (height the creature can reach without jumping) for an average creature of a given size is shown on the table below. (As a Medium creature, a typical human can reach 8 feet without jumping.)</p> 
  <p>Quadrupedal creatures don’t have the same vertical reach as a bipedal creature; treat them as being one size category smaller.</p> 
  <h4><a name="TOC-Hop-Up"></a>Hop Up</h4> 
  <p>You can jump up onto an object as tall as your waist, such as a table or small boulder, with a DC 10 Acrobatics (jump) check. Doing so counts as 10 feet of movement, so if your speed is 30 feet, you could move 20 feet, then hop up onto a counter. You do not need to get a running start to hop up, so the DC is not doubled if you do not get a running start.</p>
